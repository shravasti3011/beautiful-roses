About Roses

Roses belong to the genus Rosa and the family Rosaceae. For thousands of years, they have been cultivated not just for their breathtaking aesthetic appeal and sweet aromas, but also for their profound cultural, medical, and commercial significance worldwide.

Rose Types & Where to Find Them

Hybrid Tea Rose

The most popular class of rose, known for its large, high-centered buds and long, sturdy stems. Perfect for cut flowers.

📍 Where to find: Botanical Gardens Worldwide

Floribunda Rose

Characterized by its ability to bloom in large, vibrant clusters. They offer a continuous show of color throughout the season.

📍 Where to find: Public Parks & City Landscapes

Damask Rose

An ancient variety renowned for its deep, heavy fragrance. It is the primary choice for producing premium rose oil and water.

📍 Where to find: Kazanlak (Bulgaria), Middle East

Uses of Roses

  • Perfumes & Cosmetics: Rose essential oils and petals are structural pillars for the high-end fragrance industry and calming skincare products.
  • Culinary Delights: Rosewater, syrup, and rose hips are heavily used in culinary traditions for flavoring sweets, herbal teas, and jams.
  • Medicinal & Wellness: Rich in Vitamin C, rose hips are utilized in supplements to boost immunity and reduce inflammation.
  • Decor & Expression: Culturally irreplaceable, they serve as the universal gesture for romance, celebratory setups, and landscape design.
